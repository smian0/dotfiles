# Stock Research Question Templates

This file contains structured question templates for multi-turn stock research.

## Fundamentals Questions

1. Provide {SYMBOL}'s most recent quarterly financial results (Q3 2025): revenue, EPS, profit margins, and guidance. Compare to analyst expectations and year-over-year growth.

2. Analyze {SYMBOL}'s business model and revenue breakdown: What are the main revenue segments, geographic mix, and margin profile for each segment?

3. Compare {SYMBOL}'s valuation metrics (P/E ratio, EV/EBITDA, PEG ratio, Price-to-Sales) against industry peers and its own 5-year historical averages. Is it overvalued or undervalued?

4. Evaluate {SYMBOL}'s balance sheet strength: debt-to-equity ratio, current ratio, cash position, and free cash flow generation. How does this compare to competitors?

5. What is {SYMBOL}'s competitive position? Provide market share data, competitive advantages/moats, and how it stacks up against top 3-5 competitors.

## Catalysts Questions

1. What major news, announcements, or events have affected {SYMBOL} in the past 30 days (September-October 2025)? Include any significant price movements and their causes.

2. Analyze recent analyst activity on {SYMBOL}: rating changes (upgrades/downgrades), price target adjustments, and key institutional research reports from the past 60 days.

3. Detail any M&A activity, strategic partnerships, or major business developments announced by {SYMBOL} in 2025. What is the strategic rationale and expected impact?

4. What are {SYMBOL}'s upcoming catalysts? Include earnings dates, product launches, regulatory decisions, or other significant events in the next 3-6 months.

5. Has {SYMBOL} experienced any management changes, insider trading activity, or board-level developments in 2025? What do these signals indicate?

## Technical & Sentiment Questions

1. Analyze {SYMBOL}'s price action and technical setup: current trend, key support/resistance levels, moving averages (50-day, 200-day), and any chart patterns forming.

2. What is the institutional ownership structure for {SYMBOL}? Show recent 13F filings, major holders, and any significant changes in institutional positions (Q2-Q3 2025).

3. Examine {SYMBOL}'s insider trading activity: Have insiders been buying or selling? What does the insider sentiment indicate?

4. Analyze {SYMBOL}'s short interest, days to cover, and any changes in short positioning. Is there potential for a short squeeze or does high short interest indicate structural concerns?

5. Review {SYMBOL}'s volume trends and unusual options activity. Are there any notable patterns suggesting large institutional positioning or upcoming volatility?

## Risks Questions

1. What are the primary regulatory risks facing {SYMBOL}? Include any pending investigations, litigation, or regulatory changes that could impact the business.

2. Analyze competitive threats to {SYMBOL}: Who are the emerging competitors, what disruptive technologies could threaten the business model, and how is market share evolving?

3. What execution risks does {SYMBOL} face? Include product development timelines, integration risks from acquisitions, operational challenges, or key dependencies.

4. Present the comprehensive bear case for {SYMBOL}: Why do skeptics think it's overvalued? What could go wrong? Include valuation concerns and worst-case scenarios.

5. What macro-economic or industry-specific risks could negatively impact {SYMBOL}? Consider interest rates, economic cycles, supply chain issues, or sector-specific headwinds.

## Outlook Questions

1. What are the consensus analyst growth projections for {SYMBOL} over the next 2-3 years? Include revenue growth, EPS growth, and margin expansion expectations.

2. Analyze {SYMBOL}'s total addressable market (TAM) and growth opportunity: How large is the market, what is the company's current penetration, and what is the growth runway?

3. What are {SYMBOL}'s key strategic initiatives and how are they expected to drive future growth? Include new products, market expansion, and efficiency improvements.

4. Provide scenario analysis for {SYMBOL}: bull case (best outcome), base case (most likely), and bear case (downside scenario) with price targets and probability-weighted returns.

5. What is the long-term investment thesis for {SYMBOL}? Why would this be a compelling 3-5 year hold? What innovations or structural advantages support sustained outperformance?
